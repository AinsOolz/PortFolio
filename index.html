Travelly – Hotel Booking

This project was undertaken as part of one of my modules at Republic Polytechnic, where my team and I were tasked with developing a website of our choice. The requirements included implementing user registration and login features, along with role-based functionalities for both administrators and standard users. This particular work features a user login system, allowing users to create accounts, log in, and access different features based on their roles and an authentication system to ensure secure access.

In such collaborative work, understanding one’s role and coordinating effectively with team members becomes essential. Each individual’s contribution must integrate harmoniously with the others, ensuring that separate functions operate as a cohesive whole.

  • User registration and authentication system
  • Role-based access for administrators and standard users
  • Responsive interface designed for usability and clarity

Baker’s Bizarre Bake-Off (VR)

This project is a VR experience I developed for my final assessment in Immersive Technology. The task required the creation of an “escape room”-style environment featuring four distinct puzzles, all built around a theme of my choosing. I elected to design a whimsical, fantasy-inspired baking adventure, complete with a rotating puzzle, several logic challenges, and a finale in which the player concludes the experience by baking a magical cake. The VR project was all made using Unity and C# scripting within a span of 6 weeks.

It proved to be an unexpectedly adventurous foray into virtual reality. Throughout the module, I had to adapt to a development process far removed from my usual comfort zone, building a functional game from the ground up with limited time and resources. Though the journey was demanding, it ultimately strengthened my ability to adjust swiftly and effectively when confronted with unfamiliar challenges.

  • Development of a VR escape-room experience with multiple interactive puzzles
  • Implementation of logic-based and rotational puzzle mechanics all done in C#
  • Hands-on experience with immersive design and rapid problem-solving under constraints
